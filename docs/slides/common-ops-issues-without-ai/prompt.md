## common problems

1. Berth Planning

  1.1 issue: Inaccurate Berth planning due to inaccurate Vessel Arrival Times
      reason: Captains manually estimate ETAs and often not updated for 6–12 hours. Outdated ETAs cause berth planning to be based on inaccurate info

  1.2 issue: Berth Replanning When Vessels Arrive Late
      reason: Every vessel delay forces berth planners to manually reassign berths.
      Triggers a cascading impact on Crane scheduling and Yard positioning results in operational inefficiency


2. Crane Scheduling

issue: Vessel containers Waiting for unload
reason: During busy times, AGVs often get stuck in traffic congestion while cranes are ready to unload containers, but no AGV is available.
    Cranes sit idle waiting for transport
    Overall crane productivity is reduced


3. Fleet Management

issue: AGV wasted return Trips
reason: Most TOS in Asia assign AGV jobs reactively, one job at a time as systems do not “look ahead” to assign return jobs
AGVs often travel back empty after completing a transport job
Empty trips consume nearly as much fuel as loaded trips


4. Yard Management

issue: yard blocks becomes overcrowded
reason:
  1. Collecting (export) vessels are delayed, causing export containers to remain stuck in the yard
  2. import containers continue to unload and often stack on top of waiting export containers
  3. Retrieving buried export containers requires multiple moves per container. Increased delays and unnecessary yard movements
